## Scope and authority

P6 adds the broader research topology checkpoint: versioned landscape,
divergence, prior-art, falsification, experiment, and synthesis templates;
deterministic topology planning with dependency, budget, and concurrency gates;
authoritative topology persistence and budget lifecycle; Temporal parent/child
workflow execution and recovery; evidence-aware synthesis over admitted claim IDs
only; read-only operator projection; and a `verify:p6` acceptance gate backed by
offline adversarial fixtures.

Postgres/CAS remains authoritative for product state. Temporal remains
orchestration only. Observatory projections remain read-only and fail closed.
Cells exchange typed IDs, artifacts, contracts, and receipts rather than
unconstrained full transcripts. Consensus and model agreement remain
descriptive, not truth authority.

## Adversarial fixture contract

The frozen manifest is `evals/fixtures/p6/adversarial-manifest.json`, schema
version `1`, checkpoint `v0.6.0-research-topology`. It contains required cases
for:

- topology cycles, missing dependency nodes, unknown template versions,
  unbounded concurrency/budgets, duplicate node identities, and criterion drift;
- invalid landscape output artifacts and missing claim/evidence IDs;
- unsupported agreement, correlated consensus, admissible dissent, and retained
  unresolved issues;
- prior-art novelty status, falsification counterexample retention, and
  experiment receipt status handling;
- evidence-aware synthesis over admitted claims only;
- idle, blocked, saturated, and budget-starved scheduler states;
- duplicate topology child/budget/cancellation delivery;
- cancellation before dispatch, during cell work, after child completion, during
  synthesis, and during settlement;
- worker/client recovery, continue-as-new, replay, migration fencing, projection
  digest, and a clean multi-cell acceptance run.

`pnpm verify:p6` is non-recursive at the root and runs nine code-owned gates:

- `fixture-manifest`;
- `topology-contract-planner`;
- `scheduler-budget-policy`;
- `persistence-budget-lifecycle`;
- `postgres-topology-migration`;
- `production-profile-p6-lifecycle`;
- `temporal-execution-recovery`;
- `synthesis-provenance`;
- `projection-operator-inspection`.

The verifier also maps every required P6 adversarial case to executable proof
paths and snippets, so manifest-only coverage cannot satisfy P6.

## Review and fixes

The independent review against `9a88df1` found four blockers: P6 budget
settlement/release/cancellation duplicate stable identities could return
dishonest success, P6 authority tables lacked immutable update/delete guards,
Temporal P6 execution was only an in-process shell rather than live parent/child
workflow recovery, and `verify:p6`/fixtures were too broad to prove
production-shaped adversarial cases.

The fix response added idempotent duplicate-payload checks and zero-row
reservation transition failures in the Postgres topology repository, immutable
P6 authority-row trigger guards, a real Temporal SDK P6 parent/child workflow
with query state, child workflow IDs, continue-as-new, replay, cancellation
receipts, and worker replacement/client-handle recovery, plus stricter
executable proof mapping in `verify:p6`.

The independent re-review against `b721031a2b1b4a9038bdb132302a64d368d920c9`
reported the four blockers resolved and non-blocking. Its residual risk was
that Temporal recovery evidence uses Temporal's local test environment and
simulated worker replacement/client handle loss rather than a full external
Temporal service crash; this is accepted for P6 because the frozen plan required
durable local acceptance evidence and production service operations remain out
of scope.

PR #40 CI initially failed because adding the P6 live test to
`@mammoth/temporal-adapter test:live` violated the frozen P5 verifier's exact
live-test command. The fix restored `test:live` to the P5 command and kept
`test:p6-live` as the separate P6 gate. `verify:p5` and `verify:p6` then both
passed locally and in CI.

## Known limitations and P7 deferrals

- P6 proves the broader offline research topology and local Temporal acceptance
  gates; it does not claim managed production deployment.
- Provider-dependent live model quality remains outside offline CI.
- Managed Postgres, managed object storage, managed Temporal operations, and
  cloud egress provider/cost enforcement beyond existing policy records remain
  outside this checkpoint.
- The Observatory remains projection/read-only infrastructure, not an
  authoritative store or full 3D operator UI.
- Memory, Temporal history, model agreement, and consensus remain
  non-authoritative for truth.
