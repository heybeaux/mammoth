The Cedar Basin Experience: When Flexible Compute Firmed Up

Publisher: Plains Energy Journal (fixture trade press, independent editorial desk). Published: 2026-04-08.

Cedar Basin attracted three compute campuses in 2023 under interruptible tariffs marketed as curtailment sponges.

By 2025, two of the three had contracted firm capacity for 80 percent of their load to meet customer latency commitments, and zonal curtailment fell by only 6 percent against the 30 percent projected in the siting studies.

The county granted 620 acres of rezoning and a 45 percent property-tax abatement based on the original flexibility and curtailment projections.

A planned 200 MW battery project was cancelled after the compute campuses absorbed the interconnection headroom it had targeted, an opportunity cost documented in the utility's 2026 resource plan update.
