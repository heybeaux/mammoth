Flexible Load Interconnection Assessment

Publisher: Western Interchange Reliability Authority (fixture grid operator). Published: 2026-02-20. Assessment FL-26-02.

Flexible loads that verifiably curtail within 10 minutes can interconnect in constrained zones with limited network upgrades, reducing average interconnection study time from 31 to 9 months in the assessed queue.

Verification is the binding constraint: of 14 applicants claiming flexibility, only 5 could demonstrate contractual and telemetry-backed curtailment capability.

The mechanism works only while flexible load remains small relative to zonal curtailment: modeling shows benefits saturate when flexible compute exceeds 45 percent of average curtailed megawatts in a zone, after which additional load requires the same upgrades as inflexible load.

Uncontrolled growth of nominally flexible load that later firms up its consumption profile is identified as the principal reliability risk.
