Three Decades of Industrial Demand Response: Lessons for Compute Loads

Venue: Energy Policy Review 41(2), pages 155-190 (fixture peer-reviewed journal). Published: 2024-11-30. DOI (fixture): 10.9999/epr.2024.41.2.155.

Aluminum smelters, electrolyzers, and irrigation districts have provided interruptible load for decades; the compute co-location proposal is a new instance of a well-studied pattern, not a novel mechanism.

Historical programs show persistent incentive decay: 9 of 15 reviewed industrial interruptible programs saw participants renegotiate toward firmer service within ten years as capital utilization pressures grew.

Programs succeeded durably only where curtailment was frequent enough to stay contractually salient and where penalties for non-performance were automatic.

The review warns that high-capital-cost facilities systematically drift toward maximum utilization, which conflicts with sustained flexibility claims.
