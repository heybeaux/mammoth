Ratepayer Impact Analysis of Interruptible Data-Center Tariffs
Office of the Utility Consumer Advocate (fixture state office)
Published 2026-03-12. Report UCA-26-05.

- Interruptible tariff discounts offered to flexible data-center loads in two
  reviewed jurisdictions were funded through general rates, shifting 6 to 14
  million dollars per year to other customer classes.
- In one jurisdiction, the flexible load was curtailed only twice in 18
  months, meaning ratepayers funded a discount for flexibility that was
  rarely exercised.
- The office finds no reviewed tariff that returns transmission-deferral
  savings to host-community customers specifically; savings accrue
  system-wide while local land, water, and service burdens remain local.
- Recommendation: interruptible discounts should be funded from verified
  deferral savings, audited annually, with clawback provisions.
