Water Costs of Siting Compute in Renewable-Rich Basins

Venue: Arid Lands Research Bulletin 18(1), pages 44-67 (fixture peer-reviewed journal). Published: 2025-10-05. DOI (fixture): 10.9999/alrb.2025.18.1.44.

Renewable-curtailment hotspots in the study region overlap basins classified as over-allocated in 71 percent of mapped cases.

If facilities sited for curtailment absorption use evaporative cooling, modeled basin-level water stress increases by 4 to 12 percent in dry years, offsetting a large share of the claimed environmental benefit.

Dry cooling eliminates the water penalty but raises summer peak electricity demand precisely when curtailment is least available, creating a seasonal mismatch the co-location argument rarely addresses.

The authors conclude that curtailment-driven siting is water-safe only with dry or hybrid cooling and enforceable seasonal load commitments.
