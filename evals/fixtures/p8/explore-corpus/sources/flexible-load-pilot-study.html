Co-locating Flexible Compute with Curtailed Renewables: Pilot Results

Venue: Journal of Power Systems Integration 9(3), pages 201-226 (fixture peer-reviewed journal). Published: 2025-08-19. DOI (fixture): 10.9999/jpsi.2025.9.3.201.

A 40 MW batch-compute facility co-located at a transmission-constrained wind hub absorbed 61 percent of local curtailed energy over twelve months, raising the wind plant's effective capacity factor by 7 points.

The facility deferred 88 percent of its deferrable workload out of the top-100 net-load hours in exchange for an interruptible tariff discount.

The pilot required workloads tolerant of 4-hour interruptions; the authors caution that only an estimated 15 to 25 percent of current commercial data-center workload is deferrable at that granularity.

Onsite water use was zero: the pilot used dry cooling sized for a hot-arid site, at a measured 11 percent energy penalty during summer peaks.
