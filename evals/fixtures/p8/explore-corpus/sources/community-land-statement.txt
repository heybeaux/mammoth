Cedar Basin Rural Alliance (fixture community organization)
Position Statement on Renewable-Adjacent Compute Siting
Published 2026-03-25.

- The flexibility argument is presented as an environmental benefit, but the
  land, traffic, and noise burdens of a 300-acre campus are identical whether
  the facility is flexible or not.
- Host communities bear permanent land conversion for benefits (curtailment
  reduction, system savings) that accrue regionally or to shareholders.
- Our alliance is not opposed to compute development; we ask that flexibility
  and curtailment claims be contractually binding, independently metered, and
  publicly reported, with penalties funding local mitigation.
- Two of the three campuses in the basin have not published any verified
  curtailment performance data despite marketing claims made during
  permitting.
