Lifecycle Materials and Land Assessment of Hyperscale Campuses
Institute for Infrastructure Analysis (fixture technical institute)
Technical Report TR-2025-frozen-17. Published 2025-06-12.

Key quantified findings:

- A reference 250 MW hyperscale campus occupies 320 to 410 acres including
  substations, water infrastructure, and buffer zones.
- Construction consumes approximately 210,000 tonnes of concrete and 32,000
  tonnes of steel for the reference campus, with embodied emissions of roughly
  260,000 tonnes CO2-equivalent.
- Server refresh cycles of 4 to 5 years generate 1,900 to 2,600 tonnes of
  decommissioned IT hardware per campus per refresh; certified recycling
  streams recovered 78 percent by mass in audited programs.
- Grading and stormwater changes disturbed 100 percent of developed acreage;
  post-construction impervious cover averaged 47 percent of campus area.
- Habitat fragmentation effects extend beyond the fence line: modeled edge
  effects reduced effective contiguous habitat by 1.6 times the developed
  acreage in forested sites.
