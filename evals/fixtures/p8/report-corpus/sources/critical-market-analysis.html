Hourly Accounting of Data Center Power Claims in the Interior Plains

Publisher: Grid Integrity Project (fixture independent research group). Published: 2026-02-27.

Annual renewable-energy-certificate matching does not demonstrate carbon-free operation on an hourly basis.

Using settlement data, we find that data-center campuses in the Interior Plains balancing area drew 62 percent of their overnight electricity from gas and coal units in 2025, despite operators' annual matching claims.

Marginal-emissions analysis attributes 1.9 million tonnes CO2-equivalent in 2025 to incremental data-center load in this balancing area, emissions not reflected in operators' market-based Scope 2 disclosures.

We further estimate that without new dispatchable or storage capacity, each additional gigawatt of inflexible data-center load extends coal-unit operation in the region by 14 months on average.

Operator claims of being "powered by renewable energy" in this region are not supported by hourly settlement records.
