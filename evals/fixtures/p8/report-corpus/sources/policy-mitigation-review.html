Mitigating Data Center Impacts: Options and Open Questions

Publisher: Center for Grid Policy (fixture policy institute). Published: 2026-04-11.

Documented mitigation options include dry or hybrid cooling, hourly carbon-free energy procurement, demand flexibility commitments, large-load tariff classes with minimum-take provisions, binding community benefit agreements, and cumulative-impact permitting.

Evidence for demand flexibility is promising but thin: two pilot programs shifted 6 to 9 percent of campus load out of evening peaks, yet no independent evaluation covers sustained operation beyond one year.

Whether tax abatements are necessary to attract projects is disputed: two econometric studies reach opposite conclusions depending on how announced-but-cancelled projects are treated, and the review flags this as an open evidence gap.

Community benefit agreements produced enforceable funding in four of seven reviewed cases; the remaining three lacked enforcement mechanisms and delivered less than 30 percent of announced value.

Siting equity remains the least-studied area: the review found no peer-reviewed causal study of cumulative burden outcomes from clustered data-center development.
