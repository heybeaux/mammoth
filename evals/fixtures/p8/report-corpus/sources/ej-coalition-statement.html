Statement on Data Center Siting and Water Rights

Publisher: Twin Rivers Environmental Justice Coalition with the Kaweno Nation Environmental Office (fixture community/Indigenous organizations). Published: 2025-11-14.

Three of the four campuses permitted in the basin since 2022 are located within two miles of census tracts in the top decile of the state environmental burden index.

The Kaweno Nation holds adjudicated senior water rights in the basin; the Nation states it was not consulted before the state approved a 9,800 acre-foot annual withdrawal permit for the Cedar Flats campus in 2024.

The coalition documents that public comment periods for the last three siting decisions averaged 21 days, and technical appendices were released only in English despite 34 percent of affected-tract households speaking Spanish at home.

The coalition demands binding community benefit agreements, cumulative-impact analysis before permitting, and government-to-government consultation consistent with the Nation's treaty rights.
