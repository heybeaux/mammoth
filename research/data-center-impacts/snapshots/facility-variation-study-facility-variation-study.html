Heterogeneity of Data Center Externalities by Type, Climate, and Grid

Venue: Journal of Infrastructure Systems 31(1), pages 12-39 (fixture peer-reviewed journal). Published: 2026-01-15. DOI (fixture): 10.9999/jis.2026.31.1.12.

Externality profiles differ by an order of magnitude across facility classes: modeled water-stress-weighted consumption per megawatt-hour varies 40-fold between an air-cooled facility on a low-carbon grid and an evaporatively cooled facility on a fossil-heavy arid-region grid.

Edge and colocation facilities under 5 MW generate proportionally higher diesel-generator emissions per megawatt-hour served, because standby fleets are sized for lower reliability tiers and tested more frequently.

Hyperscale campuses achieve mean power usage effectiveness of 1.15 versus 1.6 for enterprise facilities in the study sample of 129 sites.

Lifecycle stage matters: construction-phase truck traffic, noise, and land disturbance dominate community complaints in years 0-2, while water, rates, and generator testing dominate in operating years.

The authors caution against transferring impact estimates across regions without adjusting for climate, grid mix, cooling design, and facility class.
