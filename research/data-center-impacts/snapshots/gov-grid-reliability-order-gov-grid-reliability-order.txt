FEDERAL GRID RELIABILITY COMMISSION (fixture primary regulator)
Order No. 2041 — Interconnection of Large Loads
Issued: 2026-01-22. Docket RL26-104.

1. The Commission finds that requests to interconnect single loads exceeding
   75 MW, predominantly data centers, grew from 11 in 2022 to 94 in 2025.

2. Average study time for large-load interconnection requests reached 31 months
   in 2025, and 18 regional transmission studies identified reliability
   violations attributable to concentrated new load absent network upgrades.

3. Transmission providers must file large-load tariff provisions that assign
   network upgrade costs causally to the interconnecting customer and prohibit
   socializing more than 50 percent of dedicated upgrade costs to other
   ratepayers without an explicit benefits showing.

4. Load-serving entities reported that four planned fossil retirements were
   deferred in 2025 citing forecast data-center demand growth.

Nothing in this order authorizes curtailment of retail service to existing
customers to serve new large loads.
