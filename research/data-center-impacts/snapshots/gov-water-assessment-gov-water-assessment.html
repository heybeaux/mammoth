2025 Assessment of Data Center Water Use

Publisher: National Water Resources Agency (fixture primary regulator). Published: 2025-03-18. Document ID: NWRA-2025-041.

Findings

A representative 100 MW data center using evaporative cooling consumes approximately 1.3 million cubic meters of water per year, comparable to the annual use of a town of 30,000 residents.

Of the 214 large data centers surveyed, 87 are sited in counties experiencing moderate or worse drought conditions as of the 2024 drought index.

Facilities that switched from evaporative to closed-loop air cooling reduced onsite water consumption by more than 90 percent, while increasing electricity use for cooling by 8 to 14 percent.

Thermal discharge from once-through cooling at two legacy facilities raised receiving-stream temperatures by 1.4 degrees Celsius at the mixing zone boundary.

Data limitations

Thirty-one percent of surveyed operators declined to disclose facility-level water withdrawal figures, so county aggregates rely on engineering estimates.
