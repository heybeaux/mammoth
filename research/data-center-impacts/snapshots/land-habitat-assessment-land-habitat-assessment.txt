STATE ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY DEPARTMENT (fixture state regulator)
Environmental Assessment EA-26-007 — Cedar Flats Campus Expansion
Published 2026-01-30.

- The proposed expansion converts 350 acres of mixed shortgrass prairie and
  dryland agriculture, of which 61 acres are mapped as habitat for the
  state-listed burrowing owl.
- Post-development impervious surface increases runoff volume by an estimated
  240 percent for the 10-year storm; the proposed detention design limits
  discharge to pre-development rates but requires perpetual maintenance.
- Cumulative land conversion by data-center projects in the county since 2021
  totals 1,870 acres.
- Environmental-justice screening: the affected census tracts rank in the 84th
  percentile for cumulative environmental burden; the department received 214
  public comments, 176 of which raised water or land concerns, and extended the
  comment period from 21 to 45 days in response.
- Mitigation conditions require 2:1 habitat offset acreage, seasonal
  construction windows, and annual monitoring reports for ten years.
