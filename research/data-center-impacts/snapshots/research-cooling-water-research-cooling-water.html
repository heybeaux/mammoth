Cooling Architectures and Regional Water Stress: A Comparative Study

Venue: Journal of Sustainable Computing 14(2), pages 88-114 (fixture peer-reviewed journal). Published: 2025-09-30. DOI (fixture): 10.9999/jsc.2025.14.2.88.

Across 46 modeled facility-climate pairs, switching from evaporative cooling to dry (air) cooling reduced annual onsite water consumption by 91 to 97 percent while increasing power usage effectiveness by 0.10 to 0.19.

Immersion cooling prototypes achieved water-free heat rejection at rack densities above 80 kW with a 4 to 6 percent total energy penalty in hot-arid climates.

Water-stress outcomes are dominated by siting: identical facility designs produced a 22-fold difference in water-stress-weighted impact between humid and arid grid regions.

The authors conclude that cooling-design choice cannot be evaluated apart from the local water basin and grid carbon intensity, and they publish their model inputs for replication.
