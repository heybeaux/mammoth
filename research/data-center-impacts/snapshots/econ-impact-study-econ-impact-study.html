Fiscal and Employment Effects of Data Center Campuses, 2020-2025

Publisher: Plains State University Center for Regional Economics (fixture academic center). Published: 2025-10-22. Working Paper WP-25-31, peer-reviewed 2026-01.

Across six campuses studied, construction employment averaged 1,150 worker-years per campus over 20 to 26 month build periods.

Permanent onsite employment averaged 84 jobs per campus, one third of which were filled by workers relocating from outside the county.

Net local tax revenue after abatements averaged 9.6 million dollars per campus per year; abatement agreements reduced gross property tax obligations by 47 percent on average.

Counties that negotiated community benefit agreements captured an additional 1.2 to 2.8 million dollars per year in local funding commitments.

School district revenue effects were positive in five of six cases; in the sixth, a 100 percent abatement combined with residential growth produced a net per-pupil funding decline of 3 percent.
